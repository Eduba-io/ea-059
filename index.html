// 01 · the_frame

60 / 30 / 10, applied to a Series C org.

Most organizations automate the wrong things. About 60% of daily work is traditional code and database. About 30% is rule-based logic. About 10% is genuine AI. Applied to an infrastructure company scaling its second revenue vector across five hubs, the map is specific.

60%database & code

Decisions, roadmap state, customer context, GTM playbooks, onboarding docs. Whoever answers a new hire's Thursday question is a bottleneck the org cannot afford at 400-person scale. This is a writing-down problem, not an AI problem.

30%rule_based

Qualification logic for the AI-workload buyer. When is the customer a platform-engineering lead, when is it a head of AI infra, when does the conversation shift to GPU economics. Short decision tree the seller runs in five minutes.

10%genuine_ai

The AI-workload operating model. How does the customer architect LLM inference and agent workflows on top of optimized Kubernetes, where does autonomy stop, what gets audited. This is the methodology layer. This is where Cast AI wins the expansion narrative.

// 03 · the_paper

Interpretable Context Methodology.

Submitted to ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems. Argues that organizational context can be organized as a layered filesystem (L0 identity through L4 working artifacts) with measurable gains in interpretability and reproducibility. For a 300-person company split across five hubs with two revenue vectors, ICM is not theory. It is the operating model question written out.

github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM- MIT license

Companion work: Ethics Engine (arXiv:2510.11742). A psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine. Relevant when an AI workload has to stand up to outside review.
